# Section 0 — Mathematical Foundations  
## 3. Proportionality

We consider the Universal Law of Gravitation:

$$
F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}
$$

We are asked to determine how the force $F$ changes if the distance $r$ is doubled, and both masses ($m_1$ and $m_2$) are halved.

---

## Necessary definitions and formulas

### Proportional Relationships
If a quantity depends on multiple variables, multiplying those variables by certain factors scales the entire quantity. 
For $F \propto \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$:
- $F$ is directly proportional to the product of the masses.
- $F$ is inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

---

## Step-by-step Solution

### Step 1: Define the new variables
Let the initial force be $F$. We define the new variables after the described changes:
- New distance: $r' = 2r$
- New first mass: $m_1' = \frac{m_1}{2}$
- New second mass: $m_2' = \frac{m_2}{2}$

### Step 2: Set up the new force equation
Let the new force be $F'$. We substitute the new variables into the Universal Law of Gravitation formula:

$$
F' = G \frac{m_1' m_2'}{(r')^2}
$$

### Step 3: Substitute the substituted values
Insert the expressions in terms of our original variables:

$$
F' = G \frac{\left(\frac{m_1}{2}\right) \left(\frac{m_2}{2}\right)}{(2r)^2}
$$

### Step 4: Simplify the expression
Simplify the numerator and the denominator:

- Numerator: $\left(\frac{m_1}{2}\right) \left(\frac{m_2}{2}\right) = \frac{m_1 m_2}{4}$
- Denominator: $(2r)^2 = 4r^2$

So the new equation becomes:

$$
F' = G \frac{\frac{m_1 m_2}{4}}{4r^2}
$$

Combine the fractions:

$$
F' = \frac{1}{16} \left( G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \right)
$$

Since the original force is $F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$:

$$
F' = \frac{1}{16} F
$$

---

## Final answers (summary)

The force decreases by a factor of 16. It becomes $\frac{1}{16}$ of the original force.

$$
F_{new} = \frac{F}{16}
$$
