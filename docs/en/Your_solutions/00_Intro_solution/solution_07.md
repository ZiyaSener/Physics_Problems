# Physics Problem: Logic & Series

## 1. Key Definitions and Formulas

The problem describes a bounding scenario, but it can be solved easily using constant velocity kinematics:

*   **Distance ($d$):** The total distance traveled.
*   **Speed ($v$):** The rate of change of position.
*   **Time ($t$):** The duration of the event.
*   **Kinematic Equation:** 
    $$d = v \cdot t \implies t = \frac{d}{v}$$

## 2. Solving the Problem

**Task:** Find the total distance a fly travels while bouncing between a moving bicycle ($v = 1 \, m/s$) and a wall ($10 \, m$ away) at a speed of $2 \, m/s$.

### Step-by-Step Derivation:

1.  **Determine the total duration of the event:**
    The event ends exactly when the bicycle crushes the fly against the wall. The time this takes is solely determined by the bicycle's speed and distance.
    $$t_{total} = \frac{d_{bike}}{v_{bike}}$$
    $$t_{total} = \frac{10 \, m}{1 \, m/s} = 10 \, s$$
    The entire event lasts exactly 10 seconds.

2.  **Calculate the fly's total distance:**
    The fly is in continuous motion for this entire 10-second period. Its changes in direction do not affect the total distance traveled, as distance is a scalar quantity.
    $$d_{fly} = v_{fly} \cdot t_{total}$$
    $$d_{fly} = 2 \, m/s \cdot 10 \, s = 20 \, m$$

### Final Answer:
The total distance the fly travels before being crushed is:
$$d_{fly} = 20 \, meters$$
