# Section 0 — Mathematical Foundations  
## 2. Systems of Equations

We are given the following system of linear equations:

$$
\begin{cases}
2x + 3y = 12 \\
x - y = 1
\end{cases}
$$

---

## Necessary definitions and formulas

### Substitution Method
To solve a system of linear equations using the substitution method:
1. Isolate one variable (e.g., $x$ or $y$) in one of the equations.
2. Substitute the resulting expression into the other equation.
3. Solve the resulting single-variable equation.
4. Substitute the found value back into the isolated expression to find the second variable.

---

## Step-by-step Solution

### Step 1: Isolate a variable
Looking at the second equation, isolating $x$ is straightforward:

$$
x - y = 1
$$

$$
x = y + 1
$$

### Step 2: Substitute into the first equation
Now, substitute the expression $(y + 1)$ for $x$ in the first equation:

$$
2(y + 1) + 3y = 12
$$

### Step 3: Solve for $y$
Expand the brackets and group the terms:

$$
2y + 2 + 3y = 12
$$

$$
5y + 2 = 12
$$

Subtract 2 from both sides:

$$
5y = 10
$$

Divide by 5:

$$
y = 2
$$

### Step 4: Solve for $x$
Now that we have the value of $y$, substitute it back into the equation from Step 1:

$$
x = y + 1
$$

$$
x = 2 + 1 = 3
$$

---

## Final answers (summary)

$$
x = 3, \qquad y = 2
$$
